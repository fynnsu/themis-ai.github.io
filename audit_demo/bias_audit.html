Class Distribuition

This plot can help you gain a better understanding of the distribution of your data. Having imbalanced data can impact your model negatively. For instance, if certain classes are much less populated than others it can cause your AI model to have difficulty classifying such classes resulting in loss of accuracy. Utilize the plot below to identify the under/over-represented classes. In this instance, you can observe there are far fewer even digit data points than odd digits.

Label Distribuition

Improvement Suggestions:

  • Gather more samples of even digits.
  • Use debiased training to ensure fairness of your model on all digits. (i.e. your model is not losing accuracy on even digits)

Feature Clusters

These plots cluster data points based on their features. For instance, one common feature in these clusters is the count of curves and their shapes, we can observe that the model clusters 1, 7, and 9s that look similar to each other together and 3, 8, and some 5s in another cluster. Additionally, utilize the visualizations below to identify which data points are challenging for your AI model. Additionally, this visualization helps you gain a better understanding and identify the latent features of your data. These features have the most impact on your model's prediction.

You can compare the colored by feature and colored by label plots to gain a better understanding of the difficult areas of your data.

Isolated Visualization Per Class

You can utilize these isolated plots to identify the challenging data samples from each class. The data samples are color-coded based on their difficulty for the model. Hover over the points to identify whether a data is noisy (i.e. should be removed) or under-sampled (i.e. need to gather more similar data).

Features Within Class

This plot depicts the features extracted within each class. This visualization helps you understand the distribution of your data samples under each class. Additionally, the color of each data sample represents the difficulty of that data point for your model. For instance, you can see how many different digits of 1 you have, how they are distributed, and which ones are more difficult for the model to classify. You can utilize this visualization to remove noisy data samples. Moreover, you can identify the under-represented areas to gather more data samples from.
